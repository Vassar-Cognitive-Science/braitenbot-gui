# Parts List

Everything you need to build one BraitenBot: what to buy, where to buy it, and
roughly what it costs. Many parts are sold in multi-packs, so a single robot
leaves you with spares (handy if you're building a few, or breaking a few).

:::note[Prices are approximate, *as of June 25, 2026*]

Most parts are bought from Amazon, which doesn't expose live prices to automated
tools, so the figures below are **estimates** unless marked ✓ (taken from the
linked supplier's own page). Treat them as ballpark and confirm in your cart.
Items marked † are sold in a pack whose exact count we haven't confirmed.
:::

## What it costs

| | Total |
|---|---|
| **Cost per robot** (parts actually used, packs amortized) | **≈ $124** |
| **Buy everything for one build** (one of each pack; you'll have spares) | **≈ $237** |

The gap between the two numbers is the multi-pack overhead: a single build forces
you to buy a 24-pack of batteries, a 10-pack of switches, a 20-pack of ping-pong
balls, and so on. If you're building a classroom set, the **per-robot** figure is
the one that matters: the spares get absorbed across robots.

## Notes

- **Spares after one build:** 18 batteries, 6 bump switches, 18 ping-pong balls,
  2 sensor shields, 2 color sensors, 4 servo horns, and 4 LED displays. Enough
  bump switches and displays for several more robots; the batteries alone nearly
  cover a second build.
- **The R4 Minima** is also sold by [Arduino directly](https://store-usa.arduino.cc/products/uno-r4-minima)
  for **$20.00**, cheaper than the Amazon listing if you don't mind a separate order.
- **Parts without a buy link** (screws, jumper wires, Qwiic/STEMMA QT cables) are
  generic and available from any electronics supplier; quantities and prices are
  rough.
- **Servos:** the MG996R pack ships with horns and screws, so the separate servo
  hubs are only needed if you want a specific wheel-mount style.
